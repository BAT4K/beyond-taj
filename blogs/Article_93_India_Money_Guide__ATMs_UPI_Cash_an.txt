# India Money Guide — ATMs, UPI, Cash, and Cards

**Meta description:** How money works in India for foreigners. ATMs, UPI for tourists, cash management, card acceptance, and the mistakes that cost you money.

**Target keyword:** india money guide foreigners atm upi

---

Money in India has changed dramatically. Here's the current system for foreign tourists.

## Cash Is Still King (Mostly)

Despite India's digital push, cash is needed for:
- Street vendors, small shops, auto-rickshaws without apps
- Tips (drivers, guides, hotel staff)
- Markets and bazaars
- Small restaurants and dhabas
- Rural areas

**Carry:** ₹5,000-10,000 in mixed denominations at all times. Replenish from ATMs.

## ATMs

- Available everywhere in cities. SBI, HDFC, ICICI, Axis Bank are most reliable for foreign cards.
- **Withdrawal fee:** ₹27+ per transaction at non-home-bank ATMs (since May 2025)
- **Strategy:** Withdraw ₹10,000-20,000 at once to minimize fees. Use ATMs inside bank branches (safer than standalone machines).
- **Your bank's fee:** Additional 2-3.5% foreign transaction fee from your home bank. Check before traveling.
- **Daily limit:** Usually ₹10,000-20,000 per transaction. You can do multiple transactions.

## UPI for Tourists (2026)

India's UPI (Unified Payments Interface) now works for foreign tourists from 40+ countries via **UPI One World.**
- Set up at airport or partner banks
- Scan QR codes to pay at shops, restaurants, and even street vendors
- Eliminates cash handling for most transactions
- **Recommended:** Set it up at the airport on Day 1

## Cards

- **Visa and Mastercard:** Accepted at hotels, quality restaurants, large shops, and online. Not accepted at small vendors or street food.
- **Always decline Dynamic Currency Conversion (DCC):** When a terminal asks "pay in USD or INR?" — always choose INR. DCC adds 3-5% markup.
- **Inform your bank:** Tell your bank you're traveling to India before departure. Unannounced foreign transactions often trigger fraud blocks.

## Common Money Mistakes

- Exchanging money at airport currency counters (terrible rates — use ATMs instead)
- Carrying only large bills (₹500/₹2,000 notes are hard to break at small vendors)
- Not having cash backup when cards fail (power outages kill card machines)
- Accepting torn/damaged notes (shops may refuse them — reject damaged notes from ATMs)
- Flashing money in public

## Takeaway

Withdraw cash from bank-branch ATMs in bulk. Set up UPI One World at the airport. Carry mixed denominations. Use cards at hotels and quality restaurants. Cash for everything else.
