# Took My Parents to India for Their 50th Anniversary — How to Pace It

**Meta description:** India with elderly parents requires different pacing, medical preparation, and accommodation. Here's the framework from families who've done it well.

**Target keyword:** india with elderly parents travel

---

Taking elderly parents to India is a bucket-list trip that works beautifully when the pacing is right.

## The Pacing Rules

- **3 cities maximum in 14-16 days.** No more.
- **Fly between cities.** Domestic flights are cheap (₹3,000-7,000/leg) and save hours of road fatigue.
- **One morning outing, lunch at hotel, afternoon rest, one evening outing.** That's the daily max.
- **Minimum 4 nights per base.** Single-night stops are exhausting for anyone over 65.
- **Build in 2 full rest days** across 2 weeks.

## Accessibility at Major Sites

- **Taj Mahal:** Wheelchair accessible via designated route. Electric carts available from parking to gate (₹200-300). Avoid the standard tourist queue — use wheelchair/elderly access.
- **Amber Fort Jaipur:** Elevator available (opened recently). Alternative to the steep walk.
- **Mehrangarh Fort Jodhpur:** Elevator available.
- **Humayun's Tomb Delhi:** Largely flat, wheelchair accessible.
- **Kerala houseboats:** Step-on access can be challenging for limited mobility. Choose boats with proper boarding platforms (specify when booking).

**Important:** Not all heritage hotels have elevators. Confirm before booking. Ground-floor rooms should be requested.

## Medical Preparation

- **Pre-trip doctor sign-off** with travel-medicine consultation
- **Medication list translated to Indian equivalents** — Indian pharmacies stock generic versions of most Western medications. Know the Indian brand names.
- **Hospital mapped near every hotel** — Max, Apollo, Fortis, Manipal all have international patient desks
- **Travel insurance with medical evacuation coverage** — non-negotiable for travelers over 65
- **Carry a one-page medical summary** in English: medications, allergies, conditions, emergency contacts, blood type

## Diet

- **Inform hotels in advance** about dietary needs — low-spice, low-oil, low-salt cooking is easily arranged at quality hotels
- **Carry familiar medications:** antacids, regular prescriptions, pain relievers
- **Avoid street food for elderly travelers** — stick to hotel restaurants and quality sit-down restaurants
- **Hydration matters more for elderly travelers** — India's heat dehydrates faster

## Sample Itinerary

- **Nights 1-4:** Udaipur — Lake Palace or similar heritage hotel. Gentle old-city walks. Boat rides. Rooftop dining.
- **Nights 5-8:** Jaipur — Rambagh Palace or similar. Amber Fort (elevator). City Palace. Shopping at Johari Bazaar.
- **Nights 9-10:** Agra — Taj Mahal sunrise (wheelchair access), Agra Fort. Day trip only or 1-2 nights.
- **Nights 11-13:** Delhi — Imperial Hotel or Taj Palace. Shopping at Khan Market. Farewell dinner.
- **Night 14:** Depart.

## Takeaway

India with elderly parents works beautifully at a slow pace — 3 cities, heritage hotels with elevators, flights between cities, and medical preparation. The trip becomes a gift when the parents never have to worry about logistics.
