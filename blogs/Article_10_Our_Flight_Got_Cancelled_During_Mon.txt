# Our Flight Got Cancelled During Monsoon — What to Do Next

**Meta description:** India monsoon flight cancellations happen. Here's your passenger rights under 2026 DGCA rules, step-by-step recovery protocol, and how to protect downstream bookings.

**Target keyword:** flight cancelled monsoon india what to do

---

Monsoon-related flight cancellations in India are common from June through September, especially in Mumbai, Kolkata, Goa, and Kerala. If it just happened to you, here's the protocol.

## Your Rights Under Indian Law (Updated 2026)

India's DGCA (Directorate General of Civil Aviation) rules are legally binding. As of March 2026, updated Civil Aviation Requirements give you:

**If the airline cancels your flight less than 24 hours before departure:**
- Full refund OR alternate flight on the next available service
- Monetary compensation of ₹5,000–₹10,000 depending on route length
- Free meals, hotel accommodation, and ground transport if the delay exceeds 6 hours

**If cancellation is due to "extraordinary circumstances" (monsoon weather):**
- Airlines may argue reduced compensation, but they still must offer a full refund or rebooking
- Hotel and meals are still owed if the delay is overnight

**New 2026 rule:** 48-hour penalty-free cancellation window on new bookings. If you rebook and change your mind, you can cancel free within 48 hours (if departure is 7+ days away).

**Key point:** Get the cancellation in writing from the airline — you need this for travel insurance claims and downstream rebookings.

## The First 60 Minutes

1. **Call the airline while standing in the airport queue.** Whoever gets answered first wins. IndiGo: 0124-6173838. Air India: 1800-180-1407. Vistara: 9289228888. SpiceJet: 0124-4983410.

2. **Do not leave the airport until you have a rebooking or refund confirmation.** Airlines are legally required to assist you on-site.

3. **Check other airlines immediately.** Use Google Flights or the MakeMyTrip app — a seat on another carrier may be cheaper than waiting 2 days for the original airline's next flight.

4. **Share your live location** with your hotel/tour operator at the destination so they can start adjusting.

## Protecting Downstream Bookings

This is what most travelers panic about — the hotel, the tour, the train connection that starts tomorrow.

- **Hotels:** Most Indian hotels, especially 3-star and above, do not charge no-show fees for documented flight cancellations. Call immediately, share the airline's cancellation SMS/email, ask to shift by 24-48 hours.
- **Train tickets:** Indian Railways allows cancellation with partial refund via the IRCTC app. If your train is tomorrow, you can cancel online up to 4 hours before departure.
- **Tour operators:** Contact them within 4 hours of the disruption. Reputable operators can shift itineraries by 1-2 days with minimal cost.

## Travel Insurance

If you have trip delay coverage (SafetyWing, World Nomads, Allianz all cover India), your policy typically reimburses:
- Hotel and meals during the delay
- Rebooking costs above what the airline covers
- Missed pre-paid tour costs

**File the claim within 30 days.** Keep all receipts, the cancellation confirmation, and boarding pass (even the cancelled one).

## Common Mistakes

- Standing in the airport counter queue without also calling the phone line
- Not knowing DGCA rules and accepting whatever the airline offers
- Not contacting downstream hotels/tours immediately
- Not having travel insurance with trip delay coverage
- Booking a non-refundable connection flight with a tight layover during monsoon season

## Takeaway

Monsoon flight cancellations are predictable and recoverable. Know your DGCA rights, call the airline while in queue, protect downstream bookings within 4 hours, and file insurance claims with documentation.
