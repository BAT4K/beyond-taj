# India for Digital Nomads — WiFi, Visas, Co-Working, and the Real Truth

**Meta description:** Can you actually work remotely from India? Here's the WiFi reality, visa situation, best cities, co-working spaces, costs, and the things that will drive you crazy.

**Target keyword:** india digital nomad guide

---

India is one of the cheapest places on earth to live and work remotely. The WiFi is better than you think. The visa situation is worse than you think. Here's the complete picture.

## WiFi Reality

**Mobile data:** 4G/5G coverage is excellent in all major cities and most tourist areas. Jio and Airtel offer unlimited data plans for ₹459-699/month (~$5-8). Speeds: 20-50 Mbps in cities, 5-15 Mbps in smaller towns.

**Café WiFi:** Unreliable in most of India outside dedicated co-working spaces. Don't plan to work from random cafés.

**Co-working spaces:** Available in all major cities. Speeds: 50-200 Mbps. Costs: ₹5,000-15,000/month ($60-180). Day passes: ₹300-800.

**Hotel WiFi:** Varies wildly. 4-star+ hotels usually have workable WiFi. Budget guesthouses — don't count on it.

**Best setup:** Mobile data hotspot from your phone as primary + co-working space for video calls and heavy uploads.

## The Visa Problem

India has **no digital nomad visa.** Your options:

- **e-Tourist Visa (30 days / 1 year / 5 years):** Technically for tourism only. Working remotely on a tourist visa is technically a violation — but it's widely done and rarely enforced for remote workers not employed by Indian companies.
- **Business Visa:** For people doing business with Indian entities. Doesn't cleanly cover remote work for a foreign employer.
- **The reality:** Most digital nomads use tourist visas and work remotely without issues. There has been no documented case of a foreign remote worker being deported for working on a tourist visa in India. But it's a gray area.

## What Will Drive You Crazy

- Power cuts (less common in cities, still happen — carry a laptop battery pack)
- Café WiFi dropping during video calls
- Bureaucracy for anything official (bank account, long-term accommodation)
- Noise (India is loud — noise-cancelling headphones are essential)
- The visa gray area weighing on your mind

## Takeaway

India is excellent for digital nomads who want low costs, good data, and an immersive experience. Goa and Bangalore are the strongest bases. The visa situation is gray but functional. Budget $500-1,000/month for a comfortable setup.
