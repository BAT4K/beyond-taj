# How to Get an India Visa: The Complete Foreigner's Guide (2026)

**Meta description:** Don't get scammed by fake visa websites. Here is the exact, step-by-step process for securing your Indian e-Visa safely, quickly, and securely.

**Target keyword:** how to get an india visa

---

Getting a tourist visa for India used to be a bureaucratic nightmare. Today, the process is entirely digital, fast, and relatively painless—provided you use the correct official website.

The most common mistake foreign travelers make is googling "India Visa" and clicking on the first sponsored link, which is often a predatory third-party agency that will charge you $150+ for a $25 visa.

Here is the only guide you need to secure your Indian e-Visa.

## 1. The Only Official Website

There is only **one** official Indian government website for e-Visas. 
**URL:** `https://indianvisaonline.gov.in/evisa/`

If the website does not end in `.gov.in`, you are on a third-party agency site. Close the tab immediately.

## 2. Who is Eligible?

The e-Visa facility is available to passport holders from over 165 countries, including the USA, UK, Australia, New Zealand, and most European nations. 

*Note: Due to diplomatic changes, eligibility for Canadian and UK citizens occasionally fluctuates between e-Visas and paper visas. Always check the official site for real-time updates.*

## 3. Which e-Visa Should You Choose?

India offers three types of Tourist e-Visas based on duration:

* **30-Day e-Visa:** Perfect for a single short trip. It is a double-entry visa. **Cost:** $10 (April to June) or $25 (July to March).
* **1-Year e-Visa:** Valid for 365 days from the date of grant. Multiple entries permitted (maximum stay of 90 days per visit; 180 days for US/UK citizens). **Cost:** $40.
* **5-Year e-Visa:** Valid for 5 years. Multiple entries permitted. **Cost:** $80.

*Pro Tip: For the small price difference, the 1-Year or 5-Year visas are highly recommended to avoid future paperwork if you decide to return.*

## 4. The Application Process (Step-by-Step)

1. **Prepare your documents:** You will need a digital PDF copy of your passport's bio page (under 300KB) and a square, white-background digital passport photo (under 1MB).
2. **Fill the form:** Go to the official site and click "Apply here for e-visa". The form is notoriously long. You will need details of your parents, your profession, and a "Reference Name in India" (you can use your first hotel's name and address).
3. **Pay the fee:** Payment gateways on the Indian government site are notoriously glitchy. If your credit card fails, try a different browser, clear your cache, or use PayPal (which often has a higher success rate on their portal).
4. **Wait for approval:** Approval usually takes 24 to 72 hours. You will receive an email stating your status is "GRANTED".

## 5. Arrival in India

Your e-Visa is not stamped in your passport before you leave. You must **print the ETA (Electronic Travel Authorization) document** that says "GRANTED" and carry the physical paper with you. 

When you land in Delhi, Mumbai, or any major Indian airport, follow the signs for "e-Visa Desks" (do not go to the regular immigration line). They will take your fingerprints, stamp your passport, and welcome you to India.

## The Verdict

Applying for an Indian e-Visa is cheap and straightforward if you stick to the official `.gov.in` website and have patience with their payment gateway. Apply at least two weeks before your flight to avoid any last-minute stress.
