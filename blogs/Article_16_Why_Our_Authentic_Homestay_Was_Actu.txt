# Why Our 'Authentic' Homestay Was Actually Staged — How to Tell the Difference

**Meta description:** Some Indian homestays are real family homes. Many are hotels disguised as homes. Here's how to tell before you book, and where to find the real ones.

**Target keyword:** fake authentic homestay india

---

The "authentic village homestay experience" is one of India tourism's best offerings — and one of its most frequently faked. Here's the honest taxonomy.

## The Three Types

**1. Real homestay:** A family that hosts 2-6 guests per month in their actual home. Meals cooked by the family. You sleep in their guest room. Inconsistent (some nights the food is better), social (you participate in real life), and mostly bookable through small specialized networks — not Booking.com.

**2. Hybrid homestay:** A family that has built dedicated guest cottages on their property. Interaction is real but boundaries are managed. Food is reliable. This is most of what's sold as "authentic" on big platforms. It's a legitimate product.

**3. Staged homestay:** A property dressed as a home, staffed by hired locals, marketed as authentic. The "host family" wears uniforms. Meals are restaurant-grade and identical for every guest. There's a check-in desk. There are more than 8 rooms. It's a hotel.

## How to Tell Before Booking

| Signal | Real | Staged |
|---|---|---|
| Number of rooms | 1-4 | 8+ |
| Reviews | Inconsistent (real life varies) | Uniformly 5-star |
| Booking platform | Small networks, direct referral | Booking.com, MakeMyTrip |
| Price | ₹1,500-3,500/night with meals | ₹8,000-15,000/night |
| Photos | Family's actual living space visible | Only guest cottage shown |
| Host interaction | Named family members in reviews | "Staff was friendly" language |

## Where to Find Real Homestays

- **Homestays of India** (homestaysofindia.com) — curated network
- **India Untravelled** — small-group, real family stays
- **CGH Earth properties** (Kerala) — authentic but upscale
- **Direct referrals** from experienced India travelers (Reddit r/IndiaTravel is good for this)
- **Local tourism departments** in Kerala, Himachal, Sikkim, and Nagaland run genuine homestay certification programs

## There's Nothing Wrong With Hybrid

A family that has built guest cottages is providing a real income, a real interaction, and a real cultural exchange. The problem isn't hybrid homestays — it's staged ones marketed as authentic. Know which you're booking and pay accordingly.

## Takeaway

Real homestays exist and are life-changing. Staged ones exist and are hotels. The tells are simple: room count, review consistency, price, and which platform it's listed on.
