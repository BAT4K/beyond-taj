# How to Use a Squat Toilet in India — The No-Nonsense Guide

**Meta description:** Nobody wants to ask this question. Everyone needs the answer. Here's how squat toilets work, where to find Western toilets, and what to carry at all times.

**Target keyword:** how to use squat toilet india

---

Two-thirds of the world uses squat toilets. You'll encounter them on trains, at bus stations, in smaller restaurants, and in budget accommodation. Here's the practical guide nobody wants to write.

## Where You'll Find Them

- **Train toilets** (all classes have at least one squat option)
- **Bus station restrooms**
- **Smaller restaurants and dhabas**
- **Budget guesthouses** (under ₹1,000/night)
- **Public pay-and-use toilets** (₹5-10 at stations and markets)

## Where You WON'T Find Them

- Hotels rated 3-star and above (Western toilets standard)
- Airport restrooms
- Mall restrooms
- Major restaurant chains
- Most tourist-oriented hostels (Zostel, Moustache, GoStops)

## How to Use One

1. **Face the hooded end** (the raised part with the water outlet). Your feet go on the textured footrests on either side.
2. **Pull pants/skirt to your knees or remove entirely** and hold or hang on the door hook. Do not let clothing touch the floor.
3. **Squat fully.** Don't hover — full squat is more stable and more effective. If your squat mobility is limited, practice at home before the trip.
4. **Aim for the hole.** Self-explanatory.
5. **Clean up:** Most squat toilets have a water tap with a mug or a hand spray (bum gun). Use your left hand with water to clean. Right hand stays clean. If you prefer toilet paper, bring your own — it's rarely provided.
6. **Flush:** Pull the chain/lever, or pour a bucket of water into the bowl.
7. **Wash hands thoroughly.** Carry hand sanitizer as backup.

## The Survival Kit (Carry at All Times)

- **Pocket pack of toilet paper/tissues** — not available in most squat toilets
- **Hand sanitizer** — soap may not be available
- **Wet wipes** — cleaner than dry paper in humid conditions
- **A small plastic bag** — for disposing of used paper (don't flush paper in squat toilets — it clogs them)

Total kit weight: 100g. Fits in any pocket or day bag.

## The Honest Perspective

Squat toilets are actually more hygienic than Western toilets — no seat contact means no shared surface bacteria. They're also better for your bowels (squatting position promotes more complete evacuation — this is medically documented). The discomfort is cultural, not physical. After 2-3 uses, most travelers stop thinking about it.

## If You Really Can't

- Plan your toilet stops around hotels, malls, and airports
- Major train stations have "pay and use" facilities with Western toilet options (look for the sign)
- On trains, AC First Class (1A) and AC 2-Tier (2A) have Western toilet options in addition to squat
- Carry an anti-diarrhea kit so you're never in an emergency situation without choices

## Takeaway

Squat toilets are common, hygienic, and manageable with a basic kit. Carry tissues, sanitizer, and wet wipes at all times. After day 3, you'll stop thinking about it.
