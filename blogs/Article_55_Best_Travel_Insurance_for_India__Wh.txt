# Best Travel Insurance for India — What You Actually Need

**Meta description:** Which travel insurance covers India properly, what to look for, what most policies exclude, and the specific coverage that matters for India travel.

**Target keyword:** best travel insurance india foreigners

Travel insurance for India isn't optional — it's essential. Here's what to look for and which policies actually work.

## What You Need Covered

| Coverage | Why It Matters for India | Minimum Amount |
|---|---|---|
| Medical expenses | Hospital visits, medication, doctor consultations | $100,000+ |
| Emergency evacuation | Air ambulance from remote areas (Ladakh, Himalayan treks) | $250,000+ |
| Trip cancellation/interruption | Flight cancellations (monsoon), illness, family emergency | Full trip cost |
| Trip delay | Hotel/meals during monsoon flight delays | $1,000+ |
| Baggage loss/delay | Indian domestic airlines have a mixed baggage record | $2,000+ |
| Adventure sports | If trekking, rafting, paragliding, scooter riding | Must be specifically included |

## Top Policies for India (2026)

**World Nomads** — Best for backpackers and adventure travelers
- Covers adventure sports (trekking, rafting, scooter riding)
- Medical: up to $100,000
- Trip cancellation included
- Easy to buy and extend while traveling
- Cost: ~$50-120 for 2 weeks

**SafetyWing** — Best for digital nomads and long-term travelers
- Monthly subscription model (~$45/month)
- Medical: up to $250,000
- No trip cancellation coverage (downside)
- Covers 180+ countries continuously
- Best for stays longer than 2 weeks

**Allianz Travel Insurance** — Best for families and mid-range travelers
- Comprehensive trip cancellation
- Medical: up to $250,000+
- 24/7 hotline
- Higher premiums but broader coverage
- Cost: ~$80-200 for 2 weeks

**IMG Global** — Best for medical-focused coverage
- High medical limits
- Emergency evacuation coverage excellent
- Good for travelers with pre-existing conditions (with add-on)

## What Most Policies Exclude (Read the Fine Print)

- **Pre-existing conditions** — Most standard policies exclude them. Buy an add-on if you have one.
- **Motorcycle/scooter accidents without a license** — If you rent a scooter in Goa without a valid motorcycle license, your claim will likely be denied.
- **Alcohol-related incidents** — Injuries while intoxicated are typically excluded.
- **Activities not listed as covered** — If your policy doesn't specifically mention trekking, a trekking injury may be denied.
- **Non-emergency dental** — Emergency tooth extraction yes. Filling, no.

## How to Claim

1. **Keep all receipts** — hospital bills, pharmacy receipts, taxi to hospital, hotel during delay
2. **Get a police report** for theft or crime-related claims
3. **Get written confirmation** from airline for cancellation/delay claims
4. **File within 30 days** of the incident (most policies)
5. **Photograph everything** — damaged luggage, medical documents, prescriptions

## The India-Specific Thing Nobody Mentions

Indian hospitals often require **upfront payment** for treatment — even in emergencies. Your insurance reimburses later. Carry a credit card with enough limit to cover an unexpected hospital bill (₹50,000-200,000 / $600-2,400 for a serious visit). Some premium policies offer direct hospital billing — check before you buy.

## Takeaway

Buy travel insurance before flying to India. Prioritize medical coverage ($100K+) and emergency evacuation ($250K+). World Nomads for backpackers, SafetyWing for nomads, Allianz for families. Read the adventure sports exclusions.
