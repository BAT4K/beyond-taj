# My Partner Got Sick Before Our Flight to Ladakh — What We Did

**Meta description:** The decision tree for when illness hits before a high-altitude flight. When to fly, when to postpone, and how to protect the rest of the trip.

**Target keyword:** partner sick before flight ladakh india

---

If your partner is sick and you have a flight to Ladakh (11,500 feet) tomorrow, here's the decision tree.

## Do NOT Fly If:

- **Not keeping fluids down** — altitude dehydration + GI dehydration is dangerous
- **Fever above 38.5°C** — fever at altitude is a medical red flag
- **Active diarrhea for 12+ hours** — needs antibiotics before going to altitude
- **Already dehydrated** (dark urine, dizziness standing up)

Ladakh is at 11,500 feet. The body needs to be hydrated and healthy to handle altitude. Flying sick is not just uncomfortable — it's medically risky.

## The 60-Minute Action Plan

1. **Call a doctor.** Telemedicine apps Practo and 1mg work in India. A consultation is ₹300-500 ($4-6). Available within hours, prescriptions delivered. Your hotel may also have a doctor on call.

2. **Get the cancellation letter.** Visit a hospital or clinic and get a medical letter. Indian airlines (IndiGo, Air India, Vistara, SpiceJet) allow medical rescheduling with documentation. Under 2026 DGCA rules, you can rebook with a medical certificate.

3. **Reschedule the flight.** Defer 48-72 hours minimum. Book before checking out of your current hotel, not after.

4. **Contact downstream bookings.** Hotels in Ladakh will shift your dates with a medical explanation. Most operators are accommodating with 24-48 hour notice.

All three can provide medical documentation for airline rebooking within hours.

## If the Destination Is Low-Altitude (Goa, Kerala)

The calculus is different. GI illness + sea-level destination = uncomfortable but not dangerous. Take loperamide, pack ORS, and fly. The risk of altitude is the specific problem with Ladakh.

## Takeaway

Sick + high altitude = postpone. Get a medical letter, rebook the flight, shift downstream bookings. Losing 48 hours saves the entire trip.
