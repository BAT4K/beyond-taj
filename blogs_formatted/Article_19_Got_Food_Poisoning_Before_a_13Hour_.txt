# Got Food Poisoning Before a 13-Hour Bus — The Survival Kit

**Meta description:** You can't reschedule. The bus leaves in 3 hours. Your stomach has turned. Here's the 90-minute protocol and the kit that gets you through.

**Target keyword:** food poisoning before long bus india

---

Sometimes you can't reschedule. Here's the specific protocol for getting on that bus alive and arriving functional.

## The 90-Minute Pre-Departure Protocol

**Take immediately:**
1. **Loperamide (Imodium / Eldoper):** 4mg loading dose. This slows intestinal transit. **Do not take if you have fever or bloody stools.**
2. **Ondansetron (Emeset) 4mg:** Dissolve under tongue. Stops nausea within 20 minutes.
3. **ORS (Electral):** Drink 500ml in the 90 minutes before boarding.

**Eat nothing.** Your gut needs to be as empty as possible.

## Pack the Bus Bag

- 4 more ORS sachets + water bottles (sealed)
- Extra loperamide (2mg doses for the road)
- Ondansetron (backup dose)
- Baby wipes (essential for bus bathrooms)
- 2 ziploc bags (emergency containment)
- Hand sanitizer
- A sarong (privacy at rest stops)
- Dark clothing (practical)

## On the Bus

- **Sit aisle side** if possible — easier bathroom access and exit at stops
- Indian sleeper buses stop every 3-4 hours at dhabas (highway restaurants) with bathrooms
- **Don't eat at dhaba stops** while ill — ORS only
- Bus bathrooms exist on most Volvo sleeper buses — they're rough but functional
- If symptoms worsen significantly mid-journey, get off at the next town and find a hotel. Losing one day is better than medical emergency on a highway.

## The Morning After

- Get to your hotel, shower, rest
- Plain curd rice for your first meal
- Continue ORS for 24 hours
- If symptoms persist beyond 24 hours post-bus, start azithromycin (Azithral 500mg, 1 daily for 3 days) and see a doctor

## The Kit Cost

Everything listed above costs under ₹500 ($6) at any Indian pharmacy. Assembling it takes 15 minutes. Having it before you need it is the entire point.

## Takeaway

The bus survival kit is small, cheap, and fits in a toiletry bag. Having it before the crisis hits is the difference between one bad night and a week of suffering.
