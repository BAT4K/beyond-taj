# India Travel Insurance Claim — How to Actually Get Paid

**Meta description:** Most travelers buy insurance and then can't claim because they didn't document correctly. Here's what to save, what to file, and the timeline that matters.

**Target keyword:** india travel insurance claim process

---

Buying travel insurance is the easy part. Getting paid when something goes wrong requires documentation most travelers forget to collect. Here's the checklist.

## Document Everything in Real Time

**Medical claims:**
- Hospital/clinic receipt with diagnosis in English
- Pharmacy receipts for all medications purchased
- Doctor's letter stating diagnosis and treatment
- Ambulance receipt if used
- Taxi receipt to/from hospital
- Photos of prescriptions

**Flight cancellation/delay claims:**
- Written cancellation confirmation from airline (email or SMS)
- Original boarding pass (even for the cancelled flight)
- New booking receipt
- Hotel and meal receipts during delay
- Taxi receipts during rebooking

**Theft/loss claims:**
- Police report (FIR — First Information Report) — this is MANDATORY for theft claims in India. Go to the nearest police station within 24 hours.
- List of stolen items with approximate values
- Purchase receipts for stolen items (if available)
- Hotel incident report (if theft occurred at accommodation)

**Trip interruption:**
- Medical documentation requiring early return
- Flight change receipts
- Unused accommodation booking confirmations

## The Filing Timeline

- **File within 30 days** of the incident (most policies)
- **Submit all documentation** within 60-90 days
- **Keep copies of everything** — digital and physical
- **Follow up at 2 weeks and 4 weeks** if you haven't heard back

## The Police Report (FIR) in India

For theft claims, you need a First Information Report from an Indian police station. The process:

1. Go to the nearest police station (your hotel can direct you)
2. State that you want to file an FIR for theft/loss
3. Provide your passport, describe what was stolen, state the location and time
4. Get a written, stamped copy of the FIR
5. **This can take 1-3 hours.** Be patient. Be polite. The process is bureaucratic but essential.

**Tip:** Some tourist areas have dedicated Tourist Police stations that handle foreigner reports faster.

## Common Reasons Claims Get Denied

- No police report for theft claims
- No written airline confirmation for flight delays
- Medical treatment from an unlicensed practitioner
- Scooter accident without a valid motorcycle license
- Alcohol-related incident
- Pre-existing condition not covered by policy
- Filing after the deadline
- Insufficient documentation

## Takeaway

The difference between a paid claim and a denied claim is documentation collected at the time of the incident. Keep every receipt, get the police report for theft, get written airline confirmation for delays, and file within 30 days.
