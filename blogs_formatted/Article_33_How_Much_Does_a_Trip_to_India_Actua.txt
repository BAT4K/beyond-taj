## 2-Week Trip Cost Breakdown

**Budget backpacker (2 weeks):** $400-800 per person
- Hostels/guesthouses, street food, trains (sleeper class), self-guided
- Very doable. India remains one of the cheapest countries to travel.

**Mid-range comfortable (2 weeks):** $1,500-3,500 per person
- 3-star hotels, sit-down restaurants, AC trains or domestic flights, hired guides
- This is where most foreign tourists land. Comfortable without excess.

**Luxury heritage (2 weeks):** $4,000-10,000+ per person
- Palace hotels, private drivers, heritage properties, spa treatments, private guides
- India does luxury exceptionally well for a fraction of European luxury pricing.

## Costs Most Travelers Forget

- **Foreigner entry fees.** Major monuments charge foreigners 10-20x the Indian rate. Taj Mahal: ₹1,100 for foreigners vs. ₹50 for Indians. Budget ₹8,000-12,000 for 2 weeks of sightseeing.
- **Domestic flights.** ₹3,000-8,000 ($36-96) per leg. Cheap, but adds up if you fly between 3+ cities.
- **Tips.** Budget ₹300-500/day if you're using drivers and guides.
- **SIM card.** ₹500-700 for tourist SIM with 30 days data.
- **Travel insurance.** $50-150 for 2 weeks depending on provider.
- **Visa.** $10-80 depending on type and nationality.
- **GST (goods & services tax).** 12-18% added to hotel rooms above ₹7,500/night. Often not included in quoted rates.
- **Peak season surcharge.** Hotels in Rajasthan, Kerala, and Goa charge 30-50% more during Oct-Mar. Book early.

## Money Tips

- **Use Uber/Ola** for fair transport pricing
- **ATMs:** Withdraw ₹10,000-20,000 at once to minimize per-transaction fees (₹27+ per withdrawal at non-home bank ATMs since May 2025)
- **UPI payments:** Foreign tourists from 40+ countries can now use UPI One World for digital payments — setup at airport
- **Never accept Dynamic Currency Conversion** at card terminals — always pay in INR, not your home currency
- **MRP (Maximum Retail Price):** Printed on all packaged goods. That's the max — nobody should charge more.

## Takeaway

India is extraordinarily affordable at every level. A 2-week mid-range trip costs roughly what a 3-day European city break does. Budget for foreigner entry fees, tips, and peak-season hotel premiums — those are the three costs most travelers underestimate.
