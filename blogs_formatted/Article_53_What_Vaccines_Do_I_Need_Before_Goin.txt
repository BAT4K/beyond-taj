# What Vaccines Do I Need Before Going to India?

**Meta description:** The CDC-recommended vaccines for India travel, what's essential vs. optional, when to get them, costs, and the malaria question answered honestly.

**Target keyword:** vaccines needed before india travel

---

See a travel medicine doctor 4-6 weeks before departure. Here's what they'll recommend and why.

## Essential (Recommended for ALL India Travelers)

| Vaccine | Why | When to Get | Lasts |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Hepatitis A** | Contaminated food/water. High risk in India. | At least 2 weeks before travel | 25+ years (2 doses) |
| **Typhoid** | Contaminated food/water. Common in India. | Injectable: 2 weeks before. Oral: 1 week before | Injectable: 2 years. Oral: 5 years |
| **Routine vaccines up to date** | MMR, Tetanus/Diphtheria, Polio, Flu, COVID | Check with your doctor | Varies |

**These two — Hepatitis A and Typhoid — are non-negotiable for India.** The CDC recommends both for all travelers regardless of itinerary.

## Recommended for Some Travelers

| Vaccine | Who Needs It | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Hepatitis B** | All travelers under 60; anyone at risk of blood/body fluid exposure | 3-dose series, start 6 months before OR rapid schedule available |
| **Japanese Encephalitis** | Rural areas, monsoon travel, stays longer than 1 month | 2 doses, 28 days apart. Expensive ($300-400 total) |
| **Rabies** | Rural travel, adventure activities, children (higher bite risk) | 3 doses over 21-28 days. Pre-exposure vaccination simplifies treatment if bitten |
| **Cholera** | Travelers to flood-prone or very rural areas | Oral vaccine. Moderate effectiveness |

## The Malaria Question

**Malaria exists in India** but risk varies enormously by region and season:

- **High risk:** Rural Rajasthan, Odisha, Chhattisgarh, Jharkhand, Northeast India, Goa (monsoon)
- **Low/no risk:** Urban areas (Delhi, Mumbai, Bangalore, Chennai), high altitude (Ladakh, Himachal above 2,000m), Kerala (very low risk)

**The honest answer:** Most tourists on standard routes (Golden Triangle, Kerala, Goa off-monsoon) are at low risk. Travelers going rural, trekking, or visiting during monsoon should discuss prophylaxis with their doctor.

**Prophylaxis options:** Atovaquone/Proguanil (Malarone), Doxycycline, or Mefloquine. Your doctor will recommend based on your itinerary and medical history.

**Regardless of prophylaxis:** Use mosquito repellent (DEET 30%+), sleep under treated nets in rural areas, and wear long sleeves at dusk.

## What You DON'T Need

- **Yellow Fever:** Not required for India UNLESS you're arriving from a country with yellow fever risk (parts of Africa/South America). Then a vaccination certificate is mandatory.
- **Smallpox, Plague, etc.:** Not relevant.

## Practical Tips

- **See a travel medicine specialist, not just your GP.** Travel clinics (Passport Health, local travel medicine clinics) are more up-to-date on India-specific recommendations.
- **Insurance may not cover travel vaccines.** Budget $200-500 for the full set if paying out-of-pocket.
- **Bring your vaccination card to India.** Some travel insurance claims require proof of vaccination.
- **Typhoid vaccine is not 100% effective.** Food and water hygiene still matters even with the shot.

## Takeaway

Hepatitis A and Typhoid are essential for everyone. Rabies and Japanese Encephalitis for specific itineraries. Malaria prophylaxis depends on region and season. See a doctor 4-6 weeks before flying.
