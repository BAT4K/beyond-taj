# What NOT to Do in India as a Foreigner — 12 Mistakes That Cause Real Problems

**Meta description:** Not the usual "don't touch with your left hand" list. These are the mistakes that actually ruin trips, cost money, or create unsafe situations.

**Target keyword:** what not to do india foreigner

---

Skip the list of cultural curiosities. These 12 mistakes cause real problems.

## Safety Mistakes

**1. Don't arrive at your accommodation after midnight without a confirmed booking and a phone-verified host.** Unbooked midnight arrivals at budget guesthouses in unfamiliar neighborhoods go wrong regularly.

**2. Don't walk alone in non-tourist areas after dark.** Especially in North Indian cities. This is not paranoia — it's practical risk management that applies worldwide.

**3. Don't accept food or drinks from strangers on trains.** Drugged food/drink robbery on trains is documented (rare but real). Politely decline with "thank you, I have food."

**4. Don't share your hotel name or room number with people you just met.** If asked, give a vague answer or name a different hotel.

## Money Mistakes

**5. Don't exchange money at airport currency counters.** Rates are terrible. Use bank ATMs inside the terminal.

**6. Don't pull out large bills in public.** Keep a separate pouch of small denominations (₹10, ₹20, ₹50, ₹100) for street purchases and tips. Never flash your wallet.

**7. Don't pay for anything without agreeing on price first.** Rickshaw rides, guided tours, services — agree before, not after.

## Logistics Mistakes

**8. Don't try to see too much.** The #1 trip-ruining mistake. Three cities in two weeks is perfect. Six cities in two weeks is exhausting. India rewards depth, not coverage.

**9. Don't book non-refundable everything.** Plans change in India. Weather, illness, spontaneous opportunities. Build flexibility into your bookings.

**10. Don't drive yourself.** Indian traffic operates on unwritten rules you don't know. Hire a driver or use apps. Self-driving is for experienced India residents only.

## Cultural Mistakes

**11. Don't photograph people without asking.** Especially women, children, and people bathing at ghats. A simple "photo?" with a gesture toward your camera gets consent. Most people say yes enthusiastically.

**12. Don't compare India unfavorably in front of Indians.** "In my country, this would never happen" is the fastest way to shut down goodwill. Curiosity and observation are welcomed. Judgment is not.

## Takeaway

The mistakes that actually ruin trips are structural — arriving too late, seeing too much, flashing money, driving yourself. Avoid these 12 and you've eliminated 80% of the things that go wrong.
