# My Period Started on a Train in India — What to Know

**Meta description:** What to do if your period starts unexpectedly on an Indian train. Products available, Hindi phrases that help, and a packing list every woman should carry.

**Target keyword:** period started on train india

---

One of the hardest moments on a solo trip is also one of the least written about. If your period starts unexpectedly on an Indian train, here's what to do and what to carry so it's never a crisis.

## Immediate Action on a Train

1. **Find the Train Ticket Examiner (TTE).** Every long-distance train has one. Ask: **"Mujhe sanitary pad chahiye"** (I need a sanitary pad). TTEs are trained to assist and will either help directly or locate a female staff member.

2. **Ask a female passenger.** Lean toward an older woman in your compartment and use the same phrase. Indian women are overwhelmingly helpful in these situations — female solidarity around menstruation is strong despite cultural discretion around the topic.

3. **Buy at a platform pharmacy.** Most major stations (Ratlam, Kota, Surat, Jaipur, etc.) have a pharmacy on the platform. During longer station stops (10-20 minutes), you can disembark briefly. A porter can buy supplies for you for ₹50-100 if you're uncomfortable leaving the train.

## Products Available in India

**Easy to find everywhere:**
- Pads: Whisper Choice, Stayfree, Sofy (₹30-80 per pack)
- Panty liners: Carefree (available in cities)
- ORS (Electral) for cramp-related dehydration

**Hard to find outside major cities:**
- Tampons: only reliably available in Mumbai, Delhi, Bangalore, Chennai, Hyderabad, Pune. If you use tampons, bring your own supply from home.
- Menstrual cups: increasingly stocked in pharmacy chains (Apollo Pharmacy, MedPlus) and available on Amazon India with next-day delivery in cities.

**Pain relief available OTC:**
- Ibuprofen: Brufen 400mg (₹10-15 per strip)
- Mefenamic acid: Meftal Spas (₹20-30) — specifically for menstrual cramps, very popular in India

## The Period Kit (Pack Before You Fly)

Carry this in your day bag (not checked luggage):

- 6-8 pads or your preferred product
- 2 pairs of dark underwear in a ziploc bag
- Meftal Spas or ibuprofen
- Wet wipes (unscented)
- A wrap-skirt or sarong (emergency cover-up)
- Hand sanitizer
- 2 ORS sachets
- A small ziploc for disposal (train bathrooms don't always have bins)

**Total weight: under 300g. Total preparation time: 15 minutes.**

## Cultural Context

Menstruation in India is still treated with some discretion, especially in rural areas. Some temples may restrict entry during menstruation (though enforcement toward foreigners is essentially nonexistent). Don't be surprised if discussions about periods happen in lowered voices.

That said: if you need help, ask. The discretion is cultural, the solidarity is fierce.

## Common Mistakes

- Not carrying supplies in your day bag (relying on checked luggage or hotel room)
- Assuming tampons are available outside major cities
- Being too embarrassed to ask for help on a train (every Indian woman understands)
- Not knowing the Hindi phrase

## Takeaway

A period emergency on an Indian train is solvable. Pads are available everywhere. Hindi phrases work. Other women help. The kit takes 15 minutes to assemble and lasts your entire trip.
