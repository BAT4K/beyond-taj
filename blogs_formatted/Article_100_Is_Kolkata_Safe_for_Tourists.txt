# Is Kolkata Safe for Tourists?

**Meta description:** Kolkata is safe, culturally deep, and India's most underrated major city. Here's the honest guide.

**Target keyword:** is kolkata safe tourists

---

Kolkata is safe, intellectual, and dramatically under-visited by foreign tourists.

## Safety Level: Safe

- Lower crime rate than Delhi or Mumbai
- Friendly, curious locals — Kolkata is known for its hospitality
- Iconic yellow taxis all use meters (rare in India)
- Metro system is clean and safe
- Less aggressive tout culture than North Indian tourist cities

## What to See

- **Victoria Memorial:** Stunning marble monument, excellent museum. ₹500 for foreigners.
- **Howrah Bridge:** Iconic cantilever bridge. Walk across at sunset.
- **Mother Teresa's Missionaries of Charity:** Visit the Mother House in Kolkata.
- **Kumartuli:** Artisan district where clay idols for festivals are made. Fascinating walk-through.
- **College Street:** India's largest book market. Miles of bookstalls.
- **Park Street:** Kolkata's dining and nightlife hub.

## The Food

Kolkata's food scene rivals Mumbai's. Must-try: Bengali fish curry (mustard fish), rosogolla (sweet), kathi rolls (street food wraps at Nizam's in New Market), and puchka (Kolkata's version of pani puri — arguably better).

## Honest Downsides

- Heat and humidity (especially April-September) are more intense than other Indian cities
- Tourist infrastructure is less developed than Rajasthan or Kerala
- Some colonial buildings are in poor repair
- Poverty is visible (this is true of all Indian cities but Kolkata has a specific reputation)

## Takeaway

Kolkata is safe, culturally fascinating, and has India's best street food. 2-3 days is ideal. Pair with Darjeeling (hill station, tea plantations, toy train) for a 7-day Bengal itinerary.
