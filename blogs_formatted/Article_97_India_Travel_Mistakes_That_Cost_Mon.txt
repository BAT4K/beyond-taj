# India Travel Mistakes That Cost Money

**Meta description:** The specific financial mistakes foreign tourists make in India — and the exact savings from avoiding each one.

**Target keyword:** india travel mistakes cost money

---

These mistakes cost real money. Here's each one with the specific savings.

**1. Airport currency exchange.** Rate is 5-8% worse than ATMs. On ₹50,000 exchange, you lose ₹2,500-4,000.
**Fix:** Use bank-branch ATMs inside the terminal.

**2. Accepting Dynamic Currency Conversion.** Card terminals offer to convert to your home currency at a 3-5% markup.
**Fix:** Always pay in INR. Never accept conversion.

**3. Not negotiating hotel rates for 3+ night stays.** Most hotels offer 15-25% discount for multi-night bookings if you ask.
**Fix:** Email/call directly and ask for the long-stay rate.

**4. Booking through international OTAs.** Booking.com and Expedia prices are often 10-20% higher than direct hotel rates in India.
**Fix:** Find the hotel on Booking.com, then call the hotel directly for a better rate.

**5. Paying tourist-area restaurant prices.** Restaurants within 200m of major monuments charge 2-3x normal.
**Fix:** Walk 2-3 blocks away. Prices drop dramatically.

**6. Multiple small ATM withdrawals.** Each transaction costs ₹27+ in bank fees plus your home bank's 2-3.5%.
**Fix:** Withdraw ₹10,000-20,000 at once.

**7. Buying bottled water at tourist sites.** ₹40-60/bottle vs. ₹20 at a regular shop.
**Fix:** Buy water in bulk from shops. Or use a filtered bottle.

**8. Not knowing MRP.** Every packaged product in India has a Maximum Retail Price printed on it. Nobody can legally charge more.
**Fix:** Check MRP before paying at shops.

**9. Paying for guides at the monument gate.** Unlicensed guides at gates charge ₹2,000-3,000. Licensed guides hired in advance charge ₹1,000-1,500.
**Fix:** Book guides through your hotel or operator.

**10. Tipping in foreign currency.** Your driver can't easily exchange USD/EUR notes. He gets less value.
**Fix:** Always tip in rupees.

## Total Potential Savings

A 2-week trip applying all fixes above saves approximately ₹15,000-25,000 ($180-300) compared to the uninformed tourist.

## Takeaway

The biggest money leaks are currency exchange, DCC, tourist-area restaurants, multiple small ATM withdrawals, and not booking directly. Small changes, real savings.
