# My Traveler's Diarrhea Won't Stop — What's Actually Happening

**Meta description:** If Delhi belly has lasted more than a week, it's no longer typical. Here are the 3 most common causes, the Indian pharmacy treatments, and when to see a doctor.

**Target keyword:** travelers diarrhea wont stop india

---

Standard traveler's diarrhea lasts 1-5 days. If yours has lasted more than a week, it's no longer the usual "Delhi belly." It's likely one of three specific conditions, each with a different treatment. Here's how to tell which one you have.

## The Three Common Causes

**1. Bacterial infection (most common)**
Caused by E. coli, Campylobacter, or Salmonella strains. Symptoms: persistent loose stools beyond 5-7 days, occasional low fever, fatigue, cramping.

**Treatment:** Azithromycin 500mg once daily for 3 days. Available over the counter at any Indian pharmacy under the brand name **Azithral** (Alembic Pharmaceuticals). The CDC recommends azithromycin as first-line treatment for traveler's diarrhea acquired in India and Southeast Asia.

**2. Giardia (parasitic)**
Symptoms: foul-smelling diarrhea, sulfur burps (distinctive sign), bloating, fatigue. Can last weeks if untreated.

**Treatment:** Tinidazole 2g single dose. Indian brand: **Tiniba** (Zydus). Also available OTC. One dose usually resolves it within 48 hours.

**3. Post-infectious IBS**
Symptoms: persistent loose stools after the active infection has cleared. No fever, no specific worsening triggers. Your gut microbiome was disrupted and hasn't recovered.

**Treatment:** Probiotics (VSL#3 available at Indian pharmacies), bland diet, time. Usually resolves in 2-4 weeks.

## The Pharmacy Kit You Should Already Have

Every traveler in India should carry:

- **Loperamide** (Imodium / Indian brand: Eldoper) — 4mg loading dose, then 2mg after each loose stool. Max 8mg/day OTC. Controls symptoms for bus/train travel. **Do not use if you have fever or bloody stools.**
- **Oral Rehydration Salts** — Indian brand: **Electral** or **Enerzal**. Available at every pharmacy for ₹20-30. Dissolve in 1 liter of bottled water. Drink 2-3 liters/day during active diarrhea.
- **Ondansetron** (for nausea) — Indian brand: **Emeset** 4mg. Dissolves under tongue. Stops vomiting within 20 minutes.
- **Azithromycin 500mg** x 3 tablets — for bacterial diarrhea lasting beyond 3 days.
- **Tinidazole 2g** x 1 tablet — for suspected Giardia (the sulfur-burps tell).

**Total pharmacy cost for this kit in India: ₹300-500 (under $6).**

## When to See a Doctor Instead of Self-Treating

See a doctor if you have any of:
- Blood in stool
- Fever above 39°C (102°F)
- Symptoms lasting beyond 3-4 weeks
- Signs of dehydration (dark urine, dizziness on standing, dry mouth)
- Weight loss exceeding 5%
- You've tried azithromycin and symptoms didn't improve after 72 hours

**English-speaking hospital chains across India:**

## Common Mistakes

- Taking only Imodium for weeks (this masks symptoms without treating the cause)
- Avoiding all food (you need calories — plain rice, curd, banana, toast are fine)
- Drinking only water without ORS (you're losing salts, not just water)
- Not carrying the pharmacy kit and trying to find a pharmacy while actively sick
- Ignoring Giardia symptoms (sulfur burps are a giveaway most travelers miss)

## Important Disclaimer

This article is informational, not medical advice. It's meant to help you identify what you might have and get to the right treatment faster. Always consult a healthcare professional for persistent symptoms.
