# The Kashmiri Shawl Scam That Almost Works on Everyone

The Kashmiri shawl scam is one of the most sophisticated tourist scams in India. It doesn't feel like a scam while it's happening. Here's the full script, the real tests, and where to buy safely.

## How the Scam Works (Step by Step)

1. A friendly local starts a conversation near a tourist site
2. Conversation moves to his "brother" or "cousin" who has a textile workshop
3. You're taken to an upstairs room in a shop
4. Beautiful shawls are unfurled one by one — the presentation is theater
5. "Tests" are performed: passing the shawl through a ring, holding a lighter under it, showing a microscope
6. Prices start astronomically high (₹50,000+) and "special discounts" bring them down
7. A final "family price" is offered — feels like a deal
8. You buy. You've paid $300-500 for a $30-40 acrylic blanket.

## How to Tell Real From Fake

**The burn test (most reliable):** Pull a tiny thread from the fringe. Burn it over a sink. Real Pashmina (cashmere wool) smells like burning hair and crumbles to ash. Acrylic melts into a hard plastic bead. Most shop owners won't let you do this — that itself is a tell.

**The GI tag:** Genuine Kashmiri Pashmina carries a Geographical Indication (GI) certification tag (GI No. 46) with a unique QR code or serial number verifiable online. If there's no GI tag, be skeptical.

**The touch test:** Real Pashmina feels slightly uneven with tiny bumps (handloom). Machine-made fakes feel uniformly smooth. Real Pashmina weighs 150-200g for a stole. Heavier = fillers.

**The ring test — partial truth:** Real Pashmina does pass through a wedding ring. But thin acrylic also passes. The ring test alone proves nothing.

**The price test:** Real Pashmina at fair prices:
- Stole: $150-600
- Full shawl: $400-1,500
- Kani weave shawl: $800-3,000+

If someone is selling "Pashmina" for $40-100, it's acrylic. If someone is selling it for $3,000+, you're overpaying.

## Where to Buy Safely

- **Government emporiums:** Central Cottage Industries Emporium (Delhi), state government emporiums in each state capital. Fixed prices, certified products, no bargaining needed.
- **Kashmir Loom, Srinagar** — real workshop, real artisans, real prices
- **Fabindia** — mid-range, certified products, nationwide stores
- **Online with GI verification:** Brands like Kepra and Dusala offer GI-certified products with QR code verification

## If You Already Bought One

- Do the burn test on a fringe thread at home
- Take it to any textile lab for a composition test (available in major Indian cities)
- If it's acrylic, consider it a lesson rather than a lawsuit

## Takeaway

The Kashmiri shawl scam works because the presentation is theater-grade. Know the burn test, demand the GI tag, buy from emporiums, and never follow a stranger to his "brother's shop."
