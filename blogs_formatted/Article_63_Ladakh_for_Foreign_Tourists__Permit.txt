Ladakh is India's most spectacular and most logistically demanding destination. Here's what you actually need to know.

## When to Go

**June to September only.** Mountain passes close October-May. Flights to Leh operate year-round but are unreliable in winter. The tourist season is narrow — plan accordingly.

- **June:** Passes opening, some roads still snowy. Fewer tourists.
- **July-August:** Peak season. Best weather (15-25°C in Leh). Most crowded.
- **September:** Passes start closing late month. Golden light, fewer crowds. Our pick.

## Getting There

**By air:** Direct flights from Delhi to Leh (1.5 hours). IndiGo, Air India, Vistara, SpiceJet. Book early — flights sell out in peak season. ₹5,000-15,000 one way.

**By road from Manali:** Manali-Leh Highway. 475 km, 1-2 days. Passes above 5,000m. Open June-October. Spectacular but exhausting. Not recommended for first-timers without experienced drivers.

**By road from Srinagar:** Srinagar-Leh Highway. 420 km, 1-2 days. Lower passes, slightly easier. Open May-October.

**Recommendation for first-timers:** Fly in. The road journeys are incredible but brutal — save them for trip #2.

## Altitude Sickness — Take This Seriously

Leh sits at 3,500m (11,500 feet). Altitude sickness is real and can be life-threatening.

**Mandatory acclimatization protocol:**
- **Day 1 in Leh:** Complete rest. Do not walk uphill, carry heavy bags, or sightsee. Drink 3-4 liters of water. No alcohol.
- **Day 2:** Light walking only. Short strolls in Leh town. No driving to higher passes.
- **Day 3 onwards:** Gradually increase altitude exposure.

**Symptoms of Acute Mountain Sickness (AMS):** Headache, nausea, dizziness, shortness of breath, poor sleep. Mild symptoms are common and resolve with rest and hydration.

**Red flags requiring immediate descent or hospital:** Severe headache unresponsive to paracetamol, confusion, loss of balance, persistent vomiting, fluid sounds in lungs. Go to SNM Hospital Leh immediately.

**Medication:** Acetazolamide (Diamox) 125-250mg twice daily can prevent AMS. Start 1-2 days before arriving at altitude. Consult your doctor pre-trip. Available OTC in India.

**Carry:** Pulse oximeter (₹500-1,000 on Amazon India), ORS, paracetamol, Diamox.

## Permits for Foreigners

Foreign tourists need a **Protected Area Permit (PAP)** for areas beyond Leh town — including Nubra Valley, Pangong Lake, and Hanle.

- **Apply at:** Deputy Commissioner's Office in Leh (after arriving and acclimatizing)
- **Documents:** Passport, visa, 2 photos, travel itinerary
- **Processing:** Usually same day or next day
- **Cost:** ₹400 Environmental Development Fee + ₹20/day
- **Mandatory 48-hour acclimatization in Leh** before permits are issued — this is enforced

**Tour operators can handle permits for you** — recommended for first-timers.

## What to See

- **Pangong Lake** — The iconic blue lake at 14,000 feet. 5-hour drive from Leh. Stay overnight at lakeside camps.
- **Nubra Valley** — Sand dunes, Bactrian camels, Diskit Monastery. 5-hour drive via Khardung La pass (17,982 feet).
- **Thiksey Monastery** — Stunning hilltop monastery 19 km from Leh. Morning prayers at 6 a.m. are extraordinary.
- **Hemis Monastery** — Largest monastery in Ladakh. Hemis Festival (June-July) is the biggest cultural event.
- **Hanle** — Astrophotography paradise. 14,000+ feet, zero light pollution, Indian Astronomical Observatory. Requires separate permit.
- **Zanskar Valley** — Remote, rugged, for experienced adventure travelers. Newly improved road access in 2026.

## Costs

- Flights Delhi-Leh: ₹5,000-15,000 one way
- Private car/driver: ₹3,000-5,000/day
- Accommodation: ₹1,500-5,000/night (guesthouses) to ₹10,000-25,000 (luxury camps)
- Meals: ₹300-800/day
- Permits: ₹600-800 total
- **2-week Ladakh trip budget:** $1,200-3,000 per person (mid-range)

## Common Mistakes

- Not acclimatizing (rushing to Pangong on Day 1 — dangerous)
- Driving yourself without mountain driving experience
- Not carrying altitude medication and pulse oximeter
- Visiting in April-May expecting passes to be open
- Not booking accommodation in advance during July-August

## Takeaway

Ladakh is worth every logistical challenge. Fly in, acclimatize 48 hours, get permits, and hire a local driver. The landscapes are unlike anything else on earth. Take altitude seriously.
