# The Complete India Packing List for Foreign Travelers

**Meta description:** What to actually pack, what to buy in India, what you'll wish you'd brought, and the 5 items nobody tells you about. Sorted by category.

**Target keyword:** india packing list foreign travelers

---

Pack less than you think. Buy more in India than you planned. Here's the list.

## Clothing (Pack Light, Buy Local)

- 3 loose cotton/linen pants or trousers
- 3 tops that cover shoulders (women: buy kurtas locally for ₹300-800)
- 1 long skirt (women: for South Indian temples)
- 1 light cardigan or long-sleeve layer (AC trains, religious sites)
- 1 large cotton scarf/dupatta (head cover, sun protection, temple modesty)
- 1 sarong (beach, train privacy, emergency cover-up)
- Comfortable walking shoes with back straps (you remove shoes constantly)
- Flip-flops for hostels and hotel rooms
- Underwear for 5-7 days (hand-wash or laundry is cheap — ₹50-100 for a full bag)
- 1 rain jacket (if monsoon season or mountains)

**Don't pack:** Heavy jeans (too hot), dressy shoes (unnecessary), more than 7 days of clothing (laundry is ₹50-100/bag, returned same day).

## Medical Kit (Under ₹500 at Any Indian Pharmacy)

- Loperamide (Eldoper) — anti-diarrhea
- ORS sachets (Electral) — rehydration
- Ondansetron (Emeset 4mg) — anti-nausea
- Azithromycin (Azithral 500mg x 3) — bacterial infection
- Paracetamol/ibuprofen
- Antihistamine (for allergic reactions, mosquito bite relief)
- DEET mosquito repellent (30%+)
- Sunscreen SPF 50
- Hand sanitizer (small, refillable)
- Band-aids, antiseptic cream
- Personal prescriptions (carry doctor's letter for controlled substances)

**Buy in India:** Everything above is available OTC at Indian pharmacies for a fraction of Western prices. Buy on Day 1.

## Electronics

- Universal power adapter (India uses Type C/D/M sockets — a universal adapter covers all)
- Portable battery pack (10,000+ mAh — for long train rides and power cuts)
- Noise-cancelling headphones (India is loud)
- Phone with data plan (buy Jio/Airtel SIM at airport)

## Documents

- Passport (6+ months validity, 2 blank pages)
- Printed e-Visa/ETA
- Travel insurance details (policy number, emergency phone number)
- 4 passport-size photos (for SIM purchase, permits, and emergency use)
- Digital copies of everything (email to yourself or store in cloud)
- Credit/debit card (Visa/Mastercard widely accepted in cities)
- Cash (withdraw ₹10,000-20,000 at airport ATM on arrival)

## The 5 Items Nobody Tells You About

1. **Earplugs + sleep mask** — essential for trains, noisy hotels, and early-morning temple bells
2. **Ziploc bags (10+)** — for wet clothes, protecting phone in rain/boats, organizing small items, emergency toilet paper storage
3. **Toilet paper/tissues** — not provided in most public toilets or trains
4. **Small padlock** — for hostel lockers and securing bags on trains
5. **Photocopy of passport** — carry the copy daily, leave the original in your hotel safe

## What to Buy in India (Not at Home)

- Kurtas and Indian clothing (₹300-800 each, beautiful, and you'll want them)
- Cotton scarves/dupattas (₹100-300)
- Spices as souvenirs (₹50-200/pack from spice markets)
- Ayurvedic products (₹100-500, genuine quality from pharmacy chains)
- Street snacks kit for train journeys (mixed nuts, biscuits, chai)

## Takeaway

One small backpack or rolling bag. Clothing for 5-7 days. Medical kit bought on Day 1. The items you'll use most (earplugs, ziplocs, tissues) are the ones nobody lists.
