# How to Get From Delhi Airport to the City Safely

**Meta description:** Prepaid taxis, metro, Uber/Ola, and hotel pickups — which to use, what to avoid, and the scams that target arrivals.

**Target keyword:** delhi airport to city safely

---

Your first 30 minutes in India set the tone. Here's how to get from Indira Gandhi International Airport (DEL) to your hotel without stress.

## Option 1: Prepaid Taxi Counter (Recommended for First-Timers)

Located **inside** the arrivals hall, after customs but before the exit. Look for the yellow "Delhi Traffic Police Prepaid Taxi" counter.

- Tell them your destination → they calculate the fare → you pay at the counter → receive a receipt with car number
- Fare to central Delhi (Connaught Place, Paharganj): ₹350-500
- Fare to South Delhi: ₹400-600
- Fare to Gurgaon/Gurugram: ₹500-700
- **No negotiation, no tipping, no scam risk.** Match the receipt number to the car before getting in.

## Option 2: Uber/Ola (Best for Experienced Travelers)

- Works well from Delhi airport. Pickup zone is marked outside arrivals.
- Fare to central Delhi: ₹300-500 (varies by time/surge)
- **Requires an Indian SIM with data** or airport WiFi. Buy a SIM at the telecom counters inside arrivals before going to the pickup zone.
- Match the license plate, driver photo, and car model before getting in.

## Option 3: Delhi Airport Metro Express

- Runs from Terminal 3 to New Delhi Railway Station in 20 minutes
- Operating hours: 5 a.m. to 11:30 p.m.
- Fare: ₹60
- Fast, cheap, reliable — but only useful if your hotel is near New Delhi station, Connaught Place, or metro-connected areas
- Not ideal with heavy luggage at midnight

## Option 4: Hotel Pickup (Most Comfortable)

- Most 3-star and above hotels offer airport pickup for ₹800-1,500
- More expensive but zero-stress: driver with your name on a sign, air-conditioned car, direct to hotel
- **Book in advance.** Share your flight number and arrival time.

## What to Avoid

- **Random drivers who approach you inside or outside the terminal.** They are unlicensed and will overcharge.
- **Anyone who says your hotel is closed** or offers a "better one." Your hotel is fine. Call them.
- **Anyone who offers to "help" you find the prepaid taxi counter or Uber pickup.** They're steering you to a commission driver.
- **Currency exchange inside the terminal** — rates are poor. Use ATMs instead (SBI, HDFC, ICICI ATMs are in the arrivals hall).

## The First-Timer Script

1. Land → clear immigration and customs
2. Buy Indian SIM at Jio/Airtel counter in arrivals (₹500-700, bring passport + photo)
3. Withdraw cash from bank ATM in arrivals (₹10,000-20,000 for first few days)
4. Walk to prepaid taxi counter inside arrivals hall → pay → get receipt → match car number
5. Call or WhatsApp your hotel to confirm you're on the way
6. Arrive at hotel. Breathe.

## Takeaway

Prepaid taxi counter for first-timers, Uber/Ola for experienced travelers, hotel pickup for maximum comfort. Ignore anyone who approaches you unsolicited. Your first 30 minutes are engineerable.
