# How to Travel India on a Budget — The Real Backpacker Guide

**Meta description:** India on $15-30/day is genuinely possible. Here's the specific breakdown — hostels, trains, street food, and the tricks experienced backpackers use.

**Target keyword:** travel india budget backpacker

India is one of the cheapest countries in the world to backpack. Here's how to do it on $15-30/day.

## The Daily Budget Breakdown (Per Person)

| Category | Tight Budget ($15/day) | Comfortable Budget ($30/day) |
|---|---|---|
| Accommodation | ₹300-600 (hostel dorm) | ₹800-1,500 (private room/nice hostel) |
| Food | ₹200-400 (street food + thalis) | ₹500-1,000 (mix of street + restaurants) |
| Transport | ₹100-300 (local buses, shared autos) | ₹300-800 (trains, Uber) |
| Activities | ₹50-200 (temples are free, some entry fees) | ₹200-500 (guides, entry fees) |
| **Total** | **₹650-1,500 (~$8-18)** | **₹1,800-3,800 (~$22-46)** |

## Accommodation

**Best hostel chains:** Zostel (nationwide, ₹400-800/dorm bed), Moustache Hostel (₹350-700), GoStops (₹400-900), The Hosteller (₹300-700). All have female-only dorms, lockers, common areas, and social events.

**Guesthouses:** In smaller towns without hostels, basic guesthouses cost ₹400-1000 for a private room. Ask to see the room before paying. Check for: working fan/AC, clean sheets, working bathroom lock, hot water.

**Ashrams:** Some ashrams offer free or donation-based accommodation (Rishikesh, Varanasi, Amritsar Golden Temple). You participate in community activities.

## Food

**Street thali:** A full meal (rice, dal, vegetables, roti, pickle) for ₹60-120. The best budget food in India.

**Street snacks:** Samosa ₹10-20, chai ₹10-15, pani puri ₹20-40, dosa ₹30-60.

**Restaurant meals:** Basic sit-down restaurant ₹150-300. Mid-range ₹300-600.

**Water:** ₹20 per 1-liter bottle. Buy in bulk from shops, not tourist sites.

**The rule:** Eat where locals eat. High turnover = fresh food = safe food = cheap food.

## Transport

**Trains:** Book Sleeper class (SL) for overnight journeys — cheapest option. Delhi-Jaipur SL: ~₹200. Delhi-Varanasi SL: ~₹350. Book on IRCTC.

**Buses:** State government buses are cheapest. KSRTC (Kerala), RSRTC (Rajasthan), UPSRTC (UP). ₹100-500 for most routes.

**Uber/Ola Auto:** Cheapest app option for city transport. ₹50-150 for most city rides.

**Flights:** During sales, domestic flights go as low as ₹1,500-3,000 ($18-36). Check IndiGo, SpiceJet, Air India Express. Sometimes cheaper than trains for long distances.

## Money-Saving Tricks Experienced Backpackers Use

1. **Eat thalis, not à la carte.** Thalis are unlimited refills at a fixed price. Best value in India.
2. **Travel overnight on trains.** You save a night of accommodation AND a day of travel time.
3. **Use the Foreign Tourist Quota** for train bookings — seats reserved for passport holders even when regular class is full.
4. **Visit temples instead of monuments.** Temples are free. Monuments charge foreigners 10-20x local rates.
5. **Buy a monthly SIM, not daily recharges.** Jio ₹459/28 days is cheaper than daily top-ups.
6. **Carry a reusable water bottle with filter.** LifeStraw or Grayl saves ₹40-60/day on bottled water.
7. **Negotiate accommodation for 3+ nights.** Most guesthouses offer discounts for multi-night stays.
8. **Golden Temple Amritsar = free food 24/7.** Langar (community kitchen) feeds 100,000+ people daily regardless of religion. Free.

## Common Budget Mistakes

- Flying between every city (trains are cheaper and more scenic)
- Eating at tourist-area restaurants (walk 2 blocks away, prices drop 50%)
- Buying bottled water at tourist sites (₹40-60 vs. ₹20 at a regular shop)
- Not carrying small bills (you'll overpay when vendors "can't make change")
- Booking through travel agents instead of directly

## Takeaway

India on $15-30/day is not just possible — it's comfortable. Hostel dorms, street thalis, sleeper trains, and temples. The tricks are: eat local, travel slow, book trains via IRCTC, and carry small bills.
