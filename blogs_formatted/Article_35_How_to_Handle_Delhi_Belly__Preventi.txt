# How to Handle Delhi Belly — Prevention, Treatment, and When to See a Doctor

**Meta description:** The prevention protocol, the treatment kit, what Delhi belly actually is, how long it lasts, and the specific moment to stop self-treating and see a doctor.

**Target keyword:** how to handle delhi belly

---

Around 30-50% of foreign travelers to India experience some GI trouble. Most cases resolve in 1-3 days with the right treatment. Here's the complete guide.

## What Delhi Belly Actually Is

"Delhi belly" is traveler's diarrhea — caused by exposure to bacteria, viruses, or parasites your gut hasn't encountered before. It's not about "dirty food." It's about unfamiliar bacteria. Even clean, well-prepared Indian food introduces new strains.

## Prevention (This Actually Works)

- **Drink only sealed bottled water.** Check the seal. Some vendors refill bottles with tap water.
- **No ice** unless in a quality restaurant (clear, uniform ice cubes = machine-made purified. Cloudy, irregular = tap water).
- **Wash hands or sanitize before every meal.** Carry a small hand sanitizer.
- **Eat at busy places.** High turnover = fresh food. Empty restaurant = food sitting longer.
- **Avoid raw salads and cut fruit from street vendors** in your first week. Whole fruit you peel yourself is fine.
- **Start slow.** Hotel food for 48 hours, then gradually introduce street food.
- **Probiotics:** Start taking probiotics (VSL#3 or equivalent) 3-5 days before flying to India. Continue throughout the trip. Evidence suggests this reduces GI issues.

## Treatment Kit (Under $6 at Any Indian Pharmacy)

| Medication | Indian Brand | What It Does | When to Take |
|---|---|---|---|
| Loperamide | Eldoper | Slows diarrhea | 4mg first dose, 2mg after each stool. Max 8mg/day |
| ORS | Electral, Enerzal | Replaces lost salts and fluids | Dissolve in 1L water. Drink 2-3L/day during active illness |
| Ondansetron | Emeset 4mg | Stops nausea/vomiting | Dissolve under tongue. Works in 20 min |
| Azithromycin | Azithral 500mg | Treats bacterial infection | 500mg once daily for 3 days. Start if diarrhea lasts 3+ days |
| Tinidazole | Tiniba 2g | Treats Giardia parasite | Single 2g dose. Take if sulfur burps appear |

**All available over the counter. No prescription needed.**

## The Timeline

- **Hours 0-24:** Watery diarrhea, cramps, possibly nausea. Take loperamide + ORS. Rest. Eat nothing or plain rice/curd only.
- **Hours 24-48:** If improving — continue ORS, plain food, rest. Most cases resolve here.
- **Hours 48-72:** If NOT improving — start azithromycin. Continue ORS.
- **Day 3-5:** Azithromycin should be working. If still symptomatic, see a doctor.

## See a Doctor If:

- Blood in stool
- Fever above 39°C (102°F)
- Can't keep any fluids down for 12+ hours
- Symptoms lasting beyond 5-7 days
- Sulfur burps + bloating (likely Giardia — needs tinidazole, not azithromycin)
- Signs of dehydration (dark urine, dizziness, dry mouth)

**Telemedicine in India:** Practo app, 1mg app. Consultation ₹300-500 ($4-6). Prescriptions delivered to your hotel.

## Takeaway

Delhi belly is common, usually mild, and resolves in 1-3 days with the right kit. Buy the kit on day one. Take probiotics before flying. Start slow with food. Know when to escalate to azithromycin and when to see a doctor.
