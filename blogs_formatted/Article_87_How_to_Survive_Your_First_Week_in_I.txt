# How to Survive Your First Week in India

**Meta description:** The day-by-day survival guide for your first 7 days. What hits when, what to eat, what to skip, and how to get through the adjustment.

**Target keyword:** survive first week india

---

Your first week in India follows a predictable pattern. Here's the day-by-day playbook.

## Day 1: Arrive and Rest

- Airport pickup or prepaid taxi. Go directly to hotel.
- Do NOT sightsee. Rest, hydrate, eat hotel food.
- Drink 2 liters of water. Sleep as much as possible.
- Set up Indian SIM card (buy at airport on arrival).

## Day 2: One Easy Thing

- One short walk in daylight (Lodhi Gardens if Delhi, Fort Kochi walk if Kerala).
- One sit-down restaurant meal. Nothing adventurous yet.
- Download Uber/Ola, test them with a short ride.
- Hotel food for dinner. Early sleep.

## Day 3: Start Exploring

- One monument or main attraction in the morning.
- Lunch at a recommended restaurant (ask hotel staff).
- Afternoon rest at hotel. Do NOT push through fatigue.
- First attempt at street chai — find a busy chai stall, observe locals, order "ek chai" (one tea).

## Day 4: Build Confidence

- Navigate a market or bazaar. Carry small bills. Practice "kitna?" (how much?) and "bahut zyada" (too much).
- Try one street food item from a busy stall.
- Take an auto-rickshaw (via Uber Auto for fair price).
- You're starting to read the environment now.

## Day 5: The Wall

- You may feel exhausted, emotional, or overwhelmed. This is documented culture shock.
- **Cancel plans. Rest day.** Eat familiar food. Call someone from home.
- Do NOT make any trip decisions today. The wall passes in 48 hours.

## Day 6: Recovery

- You'll feel better. The same streets that overwhelmed you will feel readable.
- Resume exploring at half-pace. One thing in the morning, one in the evening.
- Your gut has likely adjusted — broader food options open up.

## Day 7: The Shift

- You stop fighting India and start reading it. Horns become background noise.
- You navigate a taxi negotiation without anxiety.
- Someone invites you for chai and you say yes.
- The trip has started.

## Takeaway

The first week is adjustment, not vacation. Days 1-2 are rest. Day 3-4 are careful exploration. Day 5 is the wall. Day 6-7 are the shift. Plan for this rhythm and you'll arrive at week 2 ready for the real trip.
