This database is designed as a master reference for India travel planning platforms, recommendation engines, and
travel advisors. Each destination entry contains structured data fields for easy parsing and indexing.
Fields included per destination:
• Destination Name — canonical tourism name

• State / UT — Indian state or Union Territory
• Categories — all matching tourism categories (multi-valued)
• Popularity — International / Domestic / Both

• Key Attractions — 5 headline attractions

Destinations marked with multiple categories appear in their primary category section but are cross-referenced across
all applicable categories.

Leh – Ladakh
TYPE

City / Region

STATE / UT

Ladakh (UT)

POPULARITY

Both

CATEGORIES

Mountains

Leh is the gateway to the high-altitude cold desert of Ladakh, one of India's most dramatic landscapes. Situated at
3,500 m, it draws adventure travellers, bikers, and spiritual seekers with its Buddhist monasteries, turquoise lakes, and
stark Himalayan passes.
KEY ATTRACTIONS

→ Pangong Tso Lake
→ Nubra Valley & Bactrian camels
→ Thiksey & Hemis Monasteries
→ Khardung La Pass (one of world's highest motorable roads)
→ Shanti Stupa & Leh Palace
Tags: high altitude, buddhist monasteries, motorbike, adventure, cold desert, lakes, passes

Valley

STATE / UT

Accessible via the legendary Khardung La pass, Nubra Valley is a serene high-altitude desert valley where sand

roam the dunes — an almost surreal landscape.

→ Hunder sand dunes & Bactrian camel rides
→ Diskit Monastery & giant Maitreya Buddha statue
→ Panamik hot springs
→ Turtuk village (near Pakistan border)
→ Samstanling Monastery
Tags: sand dunes, camels, monastery, high altitude, remote, river valley
